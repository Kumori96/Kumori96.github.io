About Me

Kevin Chung

I’ve always surrounded myself in the tech world: building computers for fun, taking things apart, keeping up with new technology. Part of this vague passion may have been nurtured by my educational environment, in which I was given the opportunity to have great role models. Perhaps I had a natural affinity for all tech-related topics. It was not entirely clear what particular aspects of tech drew me in. Despite this lack of clarity, my interest in tech persisted and led me to pursue a degree in Computer Science at UC Davis. After receiving my education, I became more aware of what aspect of tech I truly enjoyed. Creative processes. All of my general interests had the commonality of being fundamentally creative. Now with my foundation in computer science, I plan to integrate my passion and intuition for design with my knowledge of its practicality and feasibility within the context of software engineering.

Along the way I’ve picked up various hobbies that I’m passionate about and wish to share with those who are also interested!

I’ve studied the piano for roughly 18 years with a background in classical and jazz styles. These days, I have been more focused on jazz improvisation and writing compositions inspired by various facets of my life.

I’ve also been a self-taught dancer for 6 to 7 years, exploring different styles and dance philosophies (popping, tutting, animation, krumping, c-walking, etc.). For me, dancing was a way of understanding body movement, visual expression of music, and finding confidence in portraying oneself.

Another hobby I take a great interest in is cooking. I found beauty in the complexity and harmony of bringing different flavors together and expressing a story on the plate. Creating new, enjoyable dishes and sharing them is one of my favorite ways to spend time.

Thanks for visiting!
